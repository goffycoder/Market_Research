Advanced Read

Three takeaways that matter more than the noisy month-to-month mechanics.

1. The stock matters

March current-account balances were still about 460 trillion yen, but they were roughly 71 trillion yen below a year earlier. The BOJ is shrinking the old QE footprint without draining liquidity so fast that markets buckle.

2. Fiscal timing is noisy

The April projected surplus looks large, but the source file itself says the biggest swings are mainly about treasury factors. That is not a clean signal of a policy turn.

3. Communication is shifting

The BOJ is broadening its dashboard toward underlying inflation and labor tightness. That is a direct sign it does not want markets to trade every headline CPI move as if it were the policy anchor.
